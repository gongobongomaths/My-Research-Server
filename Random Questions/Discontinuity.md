## Question: 
Let
\[
  f(x,y) = \begin{cases} 
    \frac{x^3+y^3}{x-y}, & \text{if } x\neq y \\ 
    0, & \text{if } x=y 
  \end{cases}
\]
Prove that $f$ is not continuous at $(0,0)$.

## Answer:
Take $y^3=x$, then we have the limit at $(0,0)$ via the path $y^3=x$ as follow:

$$
\begin{equation}
    \lim_{x\to 0}\frac{x^3+x}{x-x^{\frac{1}{3}}}
\end{equation}
$$
